# Finding the Perfect Conversion Factor: How I Compare Models in Minutes, Not Hours

Choosing the right conversion factor (CF) model can make or break physician compensation alignment. Get it wrong, and you risk FMV compliance issues or demotivated physicians. Get it right, and you create a win-win: fair market value compliance paired with strong productivity incentives.

The challenge? Balancing these competing priorities has traditionally required hours of manual calculations, spreadsheet modeling, and guesswork. But what if you could compare multiple CF models side-by-side and instantly see which one aligns wRVU percentiles with Total Cash Compensation (TCC) percentiles—all while staying FMV compliant?

## The Real-World Scenario

Let me walk you through a scenario I encounter regularly. I'm designing a compensation plan for a **Cardiology physician** with the following profile:

- **Specialty**: Cardiology
- **FTE**: 1.0
- **Annual wRVUs**: 6,500 (75th percentile productivity)
- **Base Salary**: $400,000
- **Market Benchmarks**: Standard cardiology survey data

The goal is straightforward: design a CF model that keeps the physician's TCC percentile aligned with their wRVU percentile (75th), stays within FMV safe zones (preferably ≤75th percentile TCC), and still provides meaningful productivity incentives.

I've created two potential models to evaluate:

**Model A: "Conservative Flat CF"**
- Single conversion factor of $58 per wRVU
- Simple, predictable structure

**Model B: "Aggressive Tiered CF"**
- Tiered model: $55/wRVU up to 5,000 wRVUs, $65/wRVU from 5,000-7,000, $75/wRVU above 7,000
- Higher rates reward increased productivity

Both models seem reasonable on paper. But which one actually delivers the alignment and FMV compliance we need?

## What Makes This Powerful

The comparison screen doesn't just show numbers—it provides context:

1. **Automatic Percentile Alignment Calculation**: The system calculates where each model places the physician relative to market benchmarks, showing wRVU percentile → TCC percentile flow with visual progress bars.

2. **FMV Risk Indicators**: Color-coded badges (LOW/MODERATE/HIGH) immediately signal compliance status. LOW risk (≤75th percentile) means no additional documentation needed. MODERATE (75-90th) requires thorough justification. HIGH (>90th) suggests reconsidering the model entirely.

3. **Alignment Status**: Each model receives an "Aligned," "Mild Drift," or "Risk Zone" designation based on the delta between wRVU and TCC percentiles. A delta under 10% is considered aligned.

4. **Smart Recommendations**: The system automatically identifies the "Best for FMV Compliance" model (lowest alignment delta within safe percentiles) and the "Best for Productivity Motivation" model (highest effective CF while staying compliant).
